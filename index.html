How the Wolverine Stack Works

BPC-157 and TB-500 operate through distinct but complementary biochemical pathways, making their combination more potent than either compound alone. BPC-157 works locally — targeting the specific site of injury — while TB-500 operates systemically, mobilizing the body's own repair resources from the inside out.

Angiogenesis & VEGF Upregulation

BPC-157 stimulates the formation of new blood vessels by increasing vascular endothelial growth factor (VEGF) expression — a critical pathway in tissue repair. Enhanced blood flow delivers oxygen and nutrients directly to damaged tissue, significantly accelerating the healing process.

Growth Factor & ERK Pathway Activation

BPC-157 upregulates pro-proliferation pathways including ERK 1/2 phosphorylation and downstream targets c-Fos, c-Jun, and Egr-1 — molecules essential for cell growth, migration, and tissue regeneration. It also increases growth hormone receptor expression in tendon fibroblasts.

Actin Regulation & Cell Migration (TB-500)

TB-500's primary mechanism centers on regulating actin — a protein essential for cell structure and movement. By binding to G-actin and promoting cytoskeletal reorganization, TB-500 enables repair cells to migrate rapidly to injury sites throughout the body.

Stem Cell Mobilization & Anti-Inflammation

TB-500 promotes the mobilization of stem and progenitor cells to form new blood vessels and regenerate tissue. It also blocks neutrophil chemotaxis and reduces macrophage infiltration, decreasing scar formation and fibrosis — complementing BPC-157's localized anti-inflammatory action.

How MOTS-C Works

MOTS-C is a 16-amino acid peptide encoded in the mitochondrial genome — specifically the 12S ribosomal RNA region. It is classified as a Mitochondrial-Derived Peptide (MDP), a relatively new category of bioactive molecules that originate in the mitochondria and act as signaling agents throughout the body. Initially researchers believed MOTS-C acted primarily within the mitochondria, but recent studies suggest it enters the bloodstream and exerts systemic effects.

MOTS-C is increasingly described as a "mitokine" — a hormone-like molecule produced by mitochondria that communicates energy status to tissues throughout the body, particularly skeletal muscle.

AMPK Pathway Activation

MOTS-C's most well-studied mechanism involves activation of AMP-activated protein kinase (AMPK) — a master regulator of cellular energy homeostasis. AMPK is activated when energy levels are low, spurring glucose and fatty acid uptake. Research suggests MOTS-C acts directly on the methionine folate cycle, increasing AICAR levels and triggering AMPK activation independent of changes in AMP/ATP ratios.

Fat Metabolism & Beta-Oxidation

In animal models, MOTS-C has been shown to regulate monoacylglycerol, dicarboxylate, and sphingolipid metabolism. By reducing activity in these pathways and enhancing beta-oxidation, it may inhibit fat accumulation. Research also suggests it increases brown adipose tissue and reduces dysfunctional adipose accumulation, potentially blocking the inflammation that follows insulin resistance.

Skeletal Muscle & Glucose Uptake

MOTS-C targets skeletal muscle, where it inhibits the folate cycle and the de novo purine biosynthesis pathway, leading to AMPK activation. This enhances skeletal muscle response to glucose uptake — importantly, in a manner independent of the insulin pathway. This offers a potential additional route for muscle cell glucose uptake even when insulin signaling is impaired.

Longevity & Stress Response

MOTS-C levels naturally decline with age, and researchers have noted correlations between MOTS-C levels and age-related metabolic decline. The peptide appears to regulate the cellular response to metabolic stress and may help counteract age-related insulin-resistant muscle changes. Its nuclear activity — particularly in response to stress — is an active area of longevity research.

How Selank Works

Selank's mechanism is multifaceted, involving at least four distinct neurochemical and immunological pathways. This polypharmacology explains both its broad therapeutic profile and its favorable side effect profile relative to classical anxiolytics. Rather than saturating a single receptor system, Selank modulates several systems at moderate intensity — producing anxiolytic and cognitive effects without the sedation or dependence associated with benzodiazepines.

GABAergic & Serotonergic Modulation

Selank's most extensively studied mechanism involves allosteric modulation of GABA-A receptors, increasing the inhibitory action of GABA — similar in some respects to benzodiazepines, but without the same receptor binding or dependence risk. Research indicates Selank influences the expression of genes involved in GABA signaling and may stimulate gene expression in neurons to affect receptor affinity for GABA. It also interacts with the serotonin system, with studies reporting increases in serotonin metabolite levels and turnover in several brain regions.

BDNF Upregulation & Neuroplasticity

Brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF) is a key protein involved in the growth, maintenance, and survival of neurons, as well as synaptic plasticity and learning. Selank has been observed to significantly increase BDNF expression in animal models — an effect not typically seen with classical anxiolytics. This upregulation may contribute to Selank's nootropic and neuroprotective properties, and distinguishes it from benzodiazepines which typically do not enhance cognitive function.

Enkephalin System Stabilization

Enkephalins are naturally occurring opioid peptides with anxiolytic properties that are rapidly degraded by enkephalinase enzymes. Research suggests elevated enkephalinase activity in animal models of anxiety and phobic disorders may deplete these natural anxiolytic peptides. Selank appears to inhibit enkephalinase, stabilizing enkephalins and resetting the enzymatic pathway — preserving the body's own natural anxiolytic mechanisms under stress conditions.

Immune & Cytokine Modulation

Derived from tuftsin — a natural immune peptide — Selank retains immunomodulatory properties. It has been studied for its potential to inhibit IL-6 gene expression in models of depression, and has been shown to affect expression of approximately 34 genes in inflammatory pathways including those affecting chemokines, cytokines, and receptors. Researchers note its adaptogenic properties may be beneficial against environmental stressors.

How Semax Works

Semax acts through multiple converging mechanisms that collectively support neuronal survival, synaptic plasticity, and neurotransmitter balance. Its most prominent feature is the absence of hormonal activity — despite being derived from ACTH, it does not stimulate the adrenal cortex or engage the HPA axis. Its biological activity is confined entirely to neurotrophic, neuroprotective, and immunomodulatory pathways.

BDNF & Neurotrophic Factor Upregulation

Semax's most well-documented mechanism is its potent upregulation of brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF) — a key protein governing neuron growth, maintenance, and survival. Research indicates Semax can increase BDNF expression significantly in the CNS, with downstream consequences including enhanced serotonin and dopamine release. This neurotrophic activity forms the foundation of its cognitive and neuroprotective effects, and has been observed in both stroke recovery and cognitive enhancement research.

Dopaminergic & Serotonergic Modulation

Through its BDNF-driven effects and direct receptor interactions, Semax modulates both the dopaminergic and serotonergic neurotransmitter systems. Studies report increases in dopamine and serotonin turnover in several brain regions. These effects contribute to Semax's reported nootropic properties — including improvements in attention, memory consolidation, and cognitive processing speed — without the stimulant profile of amphetamine-class compounds.

Neuroprotective Mechanisms

In ischemia models, Semax appears to promote the expression of 24 genes in the brain and spinal cord through various molecular mechanisms — genes believed to modulate smooth muscle cell migration, red blood cell formation, and angiogenesis. The peptide has been shown to promote neuronal survival and potentially stabilize mitochondria under hypoxic conditions. Researchers characterize its immunomodulating effect on the vascular system during ischemia as a key neuroprotective mechanism.

Immune & Anti-Inflammatory Effects

Semax carries the Pro-Gly-Pro stabilizing tripeptide appended to the ACTH core — a sequence that independently possesses anti-inflammatory properties, being itself a fragment of proline-rich immune-regulatory peptides. This contributes additional immunomodulatory activity beyond the core ACTH fragment's neurotrophic effects, particularly relevant in neuroinflammatory contexts.

How GHK-Cu Works

GHK-Cu operates through four primary mechanisms that collectively support tissue regeneration, skin repair, and cellular health. What makes GHK-Cu unusual among peptides is that its copper ion isn't merely a structural component — it actively drives enzymatic processes including superoxide dismutase activity and lysyl oxidase-mediated collagen cross-linking, giving it a dual role as both a signaling molecule and a cofactor delivery system.

Collagen & Extracellular Matrix Remodeling

GHK-Cu stimulates synthesis of collagen types I and III, elastin, proteoglycans, and glycosaminoglycans — the core structural proteins of skin and connective tissue. How Melanotan II Works

Melanotan II non-selectively binds all four major melanocortin receptors, mimicking alpha-MSH with greater potency and metabolic stability. Its ability to cross the blood-brain barrier allows effects both peripherally in the skin and centrally in the CNS.

MC1R — Melanogenesis & Skin Pigmentation

Activation of MC1R on melanocytes triggers upregulation of eumelanin — the dark pigment responsible for skin darkening. MT-II follows the same biological pathway as UV-induced tanning but without requiring UV exposure. Phase I trials showed measurable tanning after just five doses every other day.

MC4R — Appetite Suppression & Energy Balance

MC4R activation in the CNS modulates energy homeostasis and appetite. Research consistently documents reduced hunger and lower food intake. MC4R is the primary brain pathway regulating body weight and energy expenditure.

MC3R influences inflammation and immune modulation. MC5R is involved in exocrine gland secretion and skin repair — contributing to MT-II's broad physiological profile beyond pigmentation.
